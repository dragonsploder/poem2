Dust

Dust I am dust
I was dust, I am dust, and I will be dust

Dust is me
Dust endlessly spirling, glittering in the cold dark

The walls around me fade away, the lines in my mind blur into one
Dust

What is “I”, the self
Where would I end and the dust begin, if we were not together as one
One. Single. Thing.
What is a thing? A single thing. Not many parts together but one part as many.
That thing is me, it is you, it is all, it is dust

Sometimes my thoughts try to escape dust
They stream down my neurons
But they are not fast enough
To keep up with the indifference of dust
It could be anything, yet in my dust it will always be dust

Perhaps my fighting is missdirected.
Dust can not be defeated.
With dust, only more dust can be created.
Perhaps instead I should reval in the joy
Of my simplicity.
I need to exist as dust.
Not, “I am dust”
But rather just

I Am

One day,
All who care long forgotten.
But it matters not, because even without memory;

I am dust
